Vertical Formation Skydiving
============================
* Not always vertical.
* Not always skydiving.
* Always fun.

Rules
=====
More rules than you can shake a stick at.
* Indoor:  IPC or IBA?
* Outdoor: IPC or USPA?

Disciplines
===========
4-way VFS
---------
* Tunnel and sky, eight rounds, all vertical.

2-way VFS
---------
* Tunnel, two rounds of flat, four rounds of vertical.

2-way MFS
---------
* Sky, six rounds.
* Open: flat and vertical possible in the same round.
* Advanced: vertical only.

Terminology
===========
* A draw is a sequence of rounds.
* A round is a set of formations, in a specific order.
* A page is once through the formations.
* A bust is an incorrectly built formation.

Formations
----------
* A random is one point.
* A block is two points, top and bottom, with a specific inter.
* A grip may be very specific, i.e. hand to foot, not hand to leg.

Scoring
=======
* One point for each correctly performed grip.
* No point for each busted grip, as long as it was attempted.
* Incorrect or incomplete grip = bust.
* Incomplete separation between grips = bust.
* Non-stationary contact = bust.
* Omission = -3 points.
* More points in time = win.

Working Time
============
* Working time of 35 seconds.

Sky
---
"Working time starts the moment any team member (excluding the videographer) separates from the aircraft, as determined by the judges."

Tunnel 2-way
------------
"For Rookie and Intermediate when both feet of a team member leave the net and for Advanced and Open when both feet of a team member leave the floor of the antechamber and enter the wind."

Tunnel 4-way
------------
"Working time will commence the moment both feet of a single team member leave the cable net floor."

General
=======
* Slow is smooth, smooth is fast.
* Heading, level, and proximity are everything. Obsess, always.
* Know where you should be looking, always looking at something.
* Look through the center by default.
* Transition to the grip.
* Make life easy for your teammate(s).

Engineering
===========
* Dirt dive thoroughly.
* Plan your entrance / launch.
* Start with the most grips possible, within reason.
* Minimize transitions.

Grips
=====
* Fly on the correct level.
* Present in central vision.
* Know your role (don't finger fuck).
* When presenting, make a fist, be stationary!
* When closing, make it count.
